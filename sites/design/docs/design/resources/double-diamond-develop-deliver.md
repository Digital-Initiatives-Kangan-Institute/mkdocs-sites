# Double Diamond - Develop and Deliver

## Develop 
The Develop phase is the third stage of the Double Diamond, where teams generate, explore, and prototype ideas to address the problem defined earlier. It’s a highly creative and experimental stage focused on divergent thinking — encouraging many possible solutions rather than aiming for one perfect answer right away.

Teams **brainstorm, prototype, and test** rough concepts to see what works and what doesn’t. Feedback and iteration are key here — ideas are continually refined, combined, or discarded based on what’s learned.

The goal of the Develop phase is to transform insights into tangible possibilities, ready for testing and refinement in the final Deliver stage.

## Brainstorming 
**Brainstorming** is a **creative thinking technique** used to generate a wide range of ideas or solutions to a problem. The key principle is **quantity over quality at first** — encouraging open, free-flowing thinking without judging or rejecting ideas too early. 

In a typical brainstorming session, individuals or groups build on each other’s thoughts to explore as many possibilities as possible. Later, the ideas are **organized, refined, and evaluated** to find the most promising ones. 

## Prototyping 
**Prototyping** is the process of **creating a simple, early version of a product or idea** so it can be tested and improved before full development. Prototypes can take many forms — from **sketches and paper models** to **interactive digital mock-ups** or **physical builds** — depending on what’s being designed.

The goal of prototyping is to **make ideas tangible** and gather feedback quickly. By testing how users interact with a prototype, teams can **identify strengths, weaknesses, and unexpected challenges** early on.

## Testing 
**Testing** is the stage in the design thinking or **Double Diamond** process where designers **evaluate prototypes with real users** to see how well their solutions meet user needs. The goal is to learn, not just to confirm what works — by observing, listening, and gathering feedback, teams uncover what’s effective, confusing, or missing. 

Testing is an **iterative process**: insights from each test inform improvements, leading to new versions of the prototype. This cycle of feedback and refinement helps ensure the final solution is **usable, valuable, and aligned with user expectations** before it’s fully developed or implemented. 

From Testing we may need to go back to brainstorming to fix findings from the testing. 

## Deliver 
The **Deliver** phase is the fourth and final stage. It follows the **Develop** phase and focuses on finalizing, launching, and evaluating the solution.

Deliver will implement the best solution(s) that emerged from prototyping and testing, and ensure they are ready for real-world use.

Delivery includes: 
- **Final testing and validation**: Ensuring the solution meets user needs and business goals.
- **Refinement**: Making final adjustments based on feedback.
- **Launch or rollout**: Deploying the solution to users or stakeholders.
- **Evaluation and reflection**: Measuring success, gathering feedback, and identifying lessons learned.
